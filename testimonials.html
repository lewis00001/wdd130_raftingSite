Death has Dimples

Sooo … I gave this trip 1 star. Let me explain. Kim, Alexis and I wanted to do something exciting to celebrate our high school graduation. We selected a nearby rafting company and were excited to meet Sean (our super cute rafting guide). So the trip started out well and I was looking very hot in my little yellow lifevest. Did I mention that Sean had the cutest dimple? Totally cute! Anyhow, after we all got in and started down the river Sean started making jokes about our heads getting smashed my rocks. I kind of blew it off because he was likely joking, and I wanted to ask him out when we returned to the launch point - he was too cute to be creepy. I was enjoying the view of the cliffs and trees when suddenly I noticed that Sean was holding a large rock in his hand. Before I could respond Kim’s face was smashed in. Alexis and I jumped into the river and swam as fast as we could to the shore. As we struggled up the muddy bank, I looked and noticed that Sean was swimming after us. I sprinted into the woods without looking back. A moment later I heard a scream - Alexis! After a horrifying and exhausting escape, I reached an old fire road and was picked up by two great guys named Wessal and Earl. They drove me back to town. I still think Sean has a cute dimple but after this, I am not going to ask him out.

Rhonda B.

Really ... flying sharks?

I have always wanted to try white water rafting so for our 20 year anniversary my husband Phil and I booked a trip. It was going to be so wonderful. The whole rafting experience was going to be about an hour long. I was worried about piranhas or alligators in the water but our guide assured me that the worst thing we could encounter was maybe a snapping turtle, but even that was unlikely. A portion of the river was calm and I let myself relax and take in the air and sweet sunshine. Suddenly I saw something fly up over the trees on the mountain side. At first it seems like a bird but I quickly ruled that out because it was big - very big - and was twisting and flipping through the sky. I watched in shock as the thing blocked out the sun and descended upon us. Our raft was annihilated as a great white shark, with gaping mouth, slammed down upon my husband Phil. The tour guide and I were thrown into the water. He pulled me to shore and saved my life. He couldn’t save Phil. Phil now filled the belly of the shark. I will never go rafting again.
