Our Purpose

This site provides the appropriate information regarding the dangers, discomforts, and reality of whitewater rafting. Many people choose to raft even after exposure to this information. We've given adequate warning, so our hands will be washed clean in the stained water of the readers defiance and our souls vindicated by their evaporated optimism.

Reality Check

Face it, there are some basic dangers that people commonly die from. Have you considered drowning, hypothermia, overexertion, or getting your head smashed open on the rocks? Those rocks might look smooth from the riverbank, but trust us, they are still hard enough to knock you brain-dead.

It's Worse Than You Thought

Those who chose to raft are placing themselves into the hands of nature - or to be blunt - into the hands of death. There are sociopathic tour guides, sociopathic bears, raging mobs of harpooning mosquitos, beavers that deliberately drop trees across rafts, uncontacted tribes of cannibalistic spear-throwing indigenous warriors, uncharted waterfalls, rockfalls, and random sharks which fall from the sky. And what if you do not pack enough for lunch? What then? You're going to feel a little peckish and you might get hangry. Still not convinced - check out our Testimonial section for more details.
